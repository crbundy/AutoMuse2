My sisters may write to _me_. They will have       nothing else to do.”        Mr. Wickham’s adieus were much more affectionate than his wife’s.       He smiled, looked handsome, and said many pretty things.        “He is as fine a fellow,” said Mr. Bennet, as soon as they were       out of the house, “as ever I saw. He simpers, and smirks, and       makes love to us all. I am prodigiously proud of him. I defy even       Sir William Lucas himself to produce a more valuable son-in-law.”        The loss of her daughter made Mrs. Bennet very dull for several       days.        “I often think,” said she, “that there is nothing so bad as       parting with one’s friends.
