Not a word, however, passed his lips in allusion to it, till       their visitor took his leave for the night; but as soon as he was       gone, he turned to his daughter, and said:        “Jane, I congratulate you. You will be a very happy woman.”        Jane went to him instantly, kissed him, and thanked him for his       goodness.        “You are a good girl;” he replied, “and I have great pleasure in       thinking you will be so happily settled. I have not a doubt of       your doing very well together. Your tempers are by no means       unlike. You are each of you so complying, that nothing will ever       be resolved on; so easy, that every servant will cheat you; and       so generous, that you will always exceed your income.”        “I hope not so. Imprudence or thoughtlessness in money matters       would be unpardonable in _me_.”        “Exceed their income! My dear Mr. Bennet,” cried his wife, “what       are you talking of?
