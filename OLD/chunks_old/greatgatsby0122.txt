Then as Doctor T. J. Eckleburg’s faded eyes came into sight down the road, I remembered Gatsby’s caution about gasoline.  “We’ve got enough to get us to town,” said Tom.  “But there’s a garage right here,” objected Jordan. “I don’t want to get stalled in this baking heat.”  Tom threw on both brakes impatiently, and we slid to an abrupt dusty stop under Wilson’s sign. After a moment the proprietor emerged from the interior of his establishment and gazed hollow-eyed at the car.  “Let’s have some gas!” cried Tom roughly. “What do you think we stopped for—to admire the view?”  “I’m sick,” said Wilson without moving. “Been sick all day.”  “What’s the matter?”  “I’m all run down.”  “Well, shall I help myself?” Tom demanded. “You sounded well enough on the phone.”  With an effort Wilson left the shade and support of the doorway and, breathing hard, unscrewed the cap of the tank.
