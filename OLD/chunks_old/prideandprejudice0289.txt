She had not been many hours at home before she found that the       Brighton scheme, of which Lydia had given them a hint at the inn,       was under frequent discussion between her parents. Elizabeth saw       directly that her father had not the smallest intention of       yielding; but his answers were at the same time so vague and       equivocal, that her mother, though often disheartened, had never       yet despaired of succeeding at last.     Chapter 40        Elizabeth’s impatience to acquaint Jane with what had happened       could no longer be overcome; and at length, resolving to suppress       every particular in which her sister was concerned, and preparing       her to be surprised, she related to her the next morning the       chief of the scene between Mr. Darcy and herself.        Miss Bennet’s astonishment was soon lessened by the strong       sisterly partiality which made any admiration of Elizabeth appear       perfectly natural; and all surprise was shortly lost in other       feelings.
