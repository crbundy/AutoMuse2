When alone with Elizabeth afterwards, she spoke more on the       subject. “It seems likely to have been a desirable match for       Jane,” said she. “I am sorry it went off. But these things happen       so often! A young man, such as you describe Mr. Bingley, so       easily falls in love with a pretty girl for a few weeks, and when       accident separates them, so easily forgets her, that these sort       of inconsistencies are very frequent.”        “An excellent consolation in its way,” said Elizabeth, “but it       will not do for _us_.
