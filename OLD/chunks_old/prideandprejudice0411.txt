Mr.       Wickham had received his commission before he left London, and he       was to join his regiment at the end of a fortnight.        No one but Mrs. Bennet regretted that their stay would be so       short; and she made the most of the time by visiting about with       her daughter, and having very frequent parties at home. These       parties were acceptable to all; to avoid a family circle was even       more desirable to such as did think, than such as did not.        Wickham’s affection for Lydia was just what Elizabeth had       expected to find it; not equal to Lydia’s for him.
