The       party then gathered round the fire to hear Lady Catherine       determine what weather they were to have on the morrow. From       these instructions they were summoned by the arrival of the       coach; and with many speeches of thankfulness on Mr. Collins’s       side and as many bows on Sir William’s they departed. As soon as       they had driven from the door, Elizabeth was called on by her       cousin to give her opinion of all that she had seen at Rosings,       which, for Charlotte’s sake, she made more favourable than it       really was. But her commendation, though costing her some       trouble, could by no means satisfy Mr. Collins, and he was very       soon obliged to take her ladyship’s praise into his own hands.
