A thousand things may arise in six months!”        The idea of his returning no more Elizabeth treated with the       utmost contempt. It appeared to her merely the suggestion of       Caroline’s interested wishes, and she could not for a moment       suppose that those wishes, however openly or artfully spoken,       could influence a young man so totally independent of everyone.        She represented to her sister as forcibly as possible what she       felt on the subject, and had soon the pleasure of seeing its       happy effect. Jane’s temper was not desponding, and she was       gradually led to hope, though the diffidence of affection       sometimes overcame the hope, that Bingley would return to       Netherfield and answer every wish of her heart.
