What a stroke was this       for poor Jane! who would willingly have gone through the world       without believing that so much wickedness existed in the whole       race of mankind, as was here collected in one individual. Nor was       Darcy’s vindication, though grateful to her feelings, capable of       consoling her for such discovery. Most earnestly did she labour       to prove the probability of error, and seek to clear the one       without involving the other.        “This will not do,” said Elizabeth; “you never will be able to       make both of them good for anything.
