18        Till Elizabeth entered the drawing-room at Netherfield, and       looked in vain for Mr. Wickham among the cluster of red coats       there assembled, a doubt of his being present had never occurred       to her. The certainty of meeting him had not been checked by any       of those recollections that might not unreasonably have alarmed       her. She had dressed with more than usual care, and prepared in       the highest spirits for the conquest of all that remained       unsubdued of his heart, trusting that it was not more than might       be won in the course of the evening.
