Are the others coming out?”        “I do not know. Mrs. Bennet and Lydia are going in the carriage       to Meryton. And so, my dear sister, I find, from our uncle and       aunt, that you have actually seen Pemberley.”        She replied in the affirmative.        “I almost envy you the pleasure, and yet I believe it would be       too much for me, or else I could take it in my way to Newcastle.       And you saw the old housekeeper, I suppose? Poor Reynolds, she       was always very fond of me. But of course she did not mention my       name to you.”        “Yes, she did.”        “And what did she say?”        “That you were gone into the army, and she was afraid had—not       turned out well. At such a distance as _that_, you know, things       are strangely misrepresented.”        “Certainly,” he replied, biting his lips. Elizabeth hoped she had       silenced him; but he soon afterwards said:        “I was surprised to see Darcy in town last month. We passed each       other several times.
