Not Lydia only, but all were concerned       in it; and after the first exclamations of surprise and horror,       Mr. Gardiner promised every assistance in his power. Elizabeth,       though expecting no less, thanked him with tears of gratitude;       and all three being actuated by one spirit, everything relating       to their journey was speedily settled. They were to be off as       soon as possible. “But what is to be done about Pemberley?” cried       Mrs. Gardiner. “John told us Mr. Darcy was here when you sent for       us; was it so?”        “Yes; and I told him we should not be able to keep our       engagement. _That_ is all settled.”        “That is all settled;” repeated the other, as she ran into her       room to prepare. “And are they upon such terms as for her to       disclose the real truth? Oh, that I knew how it was!”        But wishes were vain, or at least could only serve to amuse her       in the hurry and confusion of the following hour.
