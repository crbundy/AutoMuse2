Were it known to the rest of my family, I should not       have merely my own gratitude to express.”        “I am sorry, exceedingly sorry,” replied Darcy, in a tone of       surprise and emotion, “that you have ever been informed of what       may, in a mistaken light, have given you uneasiness. I did not       think Mrs. Gardiner was so little to be trusted.”        “You must not blame my aunt. Lydia’s thoughtlessness first       betrayed to me that you had been concerned in the matter; and, of       course, I could not rest till I knew the particulars. Let me       thank you again and again, in the name of all my family, for that       generous compassion which induced you to take so much trouble,       and bear so many mortifications, for the sake of discovering       them.”        “If you _will_ thank me,” he replied, “let it be for yourself       alone. That the wish of giving happiness to you might add force       to the other inducements which led me on, I shall not attempt to       deny. But your _family_ owe me nothing. Much as I respect them, I       believe I thought only of _you_.”        Elizabeth was too much embarrassed to say a word. After a short       pause, her companion added,
