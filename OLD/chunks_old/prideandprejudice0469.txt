If he is satisfied with only regretting       me, when he might have obtained my affections and hand, I shall       soon cease to regret him at all.”        The surprise of the rest of the family, on hearing who their       visitor had been, was very great; but they obligingly satisfied       it, with the same kind of supposition which had appeased Mrs.       Bennet’s curiosity; and Elizabeth was spared from much teasing on       the subject.        The next morning, as she was going downstairs, she was met by her       father, who came out of his library with a letter in his hand.        “Lizzy,” said he, “I was going to look for you; come into my       room.”        She followed him thither; and her curiosity to know what he had       to tell her was heightened by the supposition of its being in       some manner connected with the letter he held. It suddenly struck       her that it might be from Lady Catherine; and she anticipated       with dismay all the consequent explanations.        She followed her father to the fire place, and they both sat       down. He then said,        “I have received a letter this morning that has astonished me       exceedingly.
