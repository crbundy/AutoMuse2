He has many       friends, and is at a time of life when friends and engagements       are continually increasing.”        “If he means to be but little at Netherfield, it would be better       for the neighbourhood that he should give up the place entirely,       for then we might possibly get a settled family there. But,       perhaps, Mr. Bingley did not take the house so much for the       convenience of the neighbourhood as for his own, and we must       expect him to keep it or quit it on the same principle.”        “I should not be surprised,” said Darcy, “if he were to give it       up as soon as any eligible purchase offers.”        Elizabeth made no answer. She was afraid of talking longer of his       friend; and, having nothing else to say, was now determined to       leave the trouble of finding a subject to him.
