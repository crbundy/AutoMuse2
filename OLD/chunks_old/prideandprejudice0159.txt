He was       anxious to avoid the notice of his cousins, from a conviction       that if they saw him depart, they could not fail to conjecture       his design, and he was not willing to have the attempt known till       its success might be known likewise; for though feeling almost       secure, and with reason, for Charlotte had been tolerably       encouraging, he was comparatively diffident since the adventure       of Wednesday. His reception, however, was of the most flattering       kind.
